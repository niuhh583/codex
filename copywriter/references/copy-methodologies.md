# Copy Methodologies

Use these methods to raise copy quality before producing final options. They are not templates to copy mechanically; they are judgment systems for creating, selecting, and improving copy.

## 1. 事实先于修辞：诚信型销售文案法

### Core judgment

Good sales copy does not win by sounding fierce. It wins by making a believable promise with facts, demonstration, empathy, and clear logic.

### Use when

- Product copy
- Detail pages
- Conversion posts
- Short video voiceover
- Sales pages
- Functional KV copy
- Promotional copy

### Rules

- First attract attention, but make sure the attention leads back to the product or problem.
- After creating interest, quickly point the reader toward the product value.
- Write for the real buyer, not for everyone.
- Show product performance instead of only claiming superiority.
- Prefer facts over adjectives, and demonstrations over slogans.
- Build desire from real needs, scarcity, self-image, or emotional projection.
- Earn credibility by proving that the brand understands the user's problem.
- Challenge category assumptions only when supported by facts or a new perspective.
- Turn facts into a proposition; do not dress unsupported claims as facts.

### Avoid

- Attention tricks unrelated to the product.
- Empty claims such as better, stronger, more premium, or more advanced.
- Treating consumers as targets to conquer instead of people to understand.
- Artistic self-expression that does not move the reader toward action.

## 2. 先准后狠：产品定义、承诺提炼与标题爆破法

### Core judgment

The force of copy comes after accuracy. First define the product, the buyer, the promise, and the proof. Then write sharp headlines and memorable expressions.

### Use when

- Brand advertising
- Product advertising
- Long-form conversion copy
- Social selling posts
- KV headlines
- Detail pages
- Event promotion
- Short video hooks

### Rules

- Describe the product in 20-30 words before writing: feature, function, target consumer, and emotional or spiritual benefit.
- Define the promise to the consumer. The more specific the promise, the stronger it is.
- Do not write a promise that you cannot believe or prove.
- Establish a simple, original, extendable Big Idea before writing executions.
- Before drafting body copy, write at least 15 headline options.
- Create headlines through story, novelty, or newsworthiness.
- Use creative methods such as personification, reverse thinking, scenario imagination, cultural or news hooks, metaphor, symbol, association, exclamation, layout ideas, and familiar cues.
- Body copy should use facts, numbers, true experience, and concrete benefits.
- Address the target audience directly.
- Let length follow the sales task. Short is not always better; clear and persuasive is better.
- Think visually while writing so the copy can work with design.
- Focus one ad on one main promise.
- Edit like a news editor: cut, arrange, and place the strongest information where it will be seen.

### Avoid

- Writing pretty sentences first and reverse-engineering the strategy later.
- Using abstract words instead of concrete promises.
- Stacking too many selling points in one execution.
- Using obscure words or complicated phrasing.
- Letting novelty pull the headline away from the product.

## 3. 标题先行：社媒点击触发器与兑现法

### Core judgment

A social title is not a summary. It is a click decision trigger. It must reduce comprehension cost, activate a psychological motive, and give the user a reason to enter the content.

### Use when

- Xiaohongshu titles
- WeChat article titles
- Short video cover copy
- Social post openings
- Content marketing
- Seed posts
- Event posts

### Rules

- Use numbers to create certainty only when the number is true and useful.
- Use trends only when they connect to brand, product, or audience value.
- Hit emotion first: anxiety, exhaustion, desire, relief, grievance, clarity, or being seen.
- Leave a curiosity gap tied to the actual content.
- Create contrast between two states, such as effort versus no result, recovery versus loss of control, or low price versus high value.
- State the benefit clearly: save time, avoid mistakes, learn something, feel better, choose better.
- Use specific identity labels so the reader feels addressed.
- Ask questions that already exist in the user's mind.
- Add scenes, actions, time, body feelings, or life details.
- Use reverse expressions such as do not, stop, not recommended, or never only when the article can support the claim.
- The title must be fulfilled by the content.
- Each title should have at least one clear psychological trigger.

### Avoid

- Clickbait that the content cannot deliver.
- Fake numbers.
- Trend-jacking without relevance.
- Emotion without value.
- Suspense disconnected from the content.
- Generic formulas such as must read, save this, or too good without a real angle.

## 4. 作者性文案法：人设、分寸与脾气

### Core judgment

Copy with no AI taste needs a clear speaking subject. Before writing, define who is speaking, from what position, with what personality, limits, and value stance.

### Use when

- Brand manifesto
- Brand attitude copy
- Social content systems
- Founder brands
- Lifestyle brands
- Cultural brands
- Product launch copy
- Value statements
- Crisis or response copy

### Rules

- Define the brand's speaking subject: what kind of person does the brand resemble?
- Before writing, answer: who is speaking, from what stance, and what expression must be made?
- Let brand personality come from founder spirit, product traits, user interaction, service behavior, team habits, and long-term brand accumulation.
- Build personality from both brand truth and the user's ideal self.
- Keep the writer's ego smaller than the brand, product, and service.
- Start from product or business traits, find the shared feeling with the user, then express it through the brand's value stance.
- Give the brand a point of view; it does not need to please everyone.
- Every expression should accumulate brand tone, not only chase a topic.
- The tone can be gentle, sharp, plain, calm, rebellious, or restrained, but it must serve the same brand core.
- Test authorship by asking: if the logo is removed, can the copy still feel like this brand?

### Avoid

- Mistaking casual language for humanity.
- Forcing emotion or golden sentences just to avoid AI taste.
- Letting the writer's personality cover the brand.
- Changing brand personality with every trend.
- Imitating another brand's tone without your own speaking subject.
- Becoming average, templated, or safe.

## 5. 文化转译文案法：商品、语境与身份想象

### Core judgment

High-level brand copy does not only introduce a product. It places the product inside culture, desire, identity, and a way of living.

### Use when

- Brand manifesto
- Lifestyle brands
- Bookstores
- Art spaces
- Exhibitions
- Premium retail
- City commercial spaces
- Brand events
- Premium personal care
- Fragrance, fashion, and aesthetic consumption brands

### Rules

- Do not only write function; write the lifestyle, desire, and identity carried by the product.
- Place the product inside cultural coordinates: literature, film, art, philosophy, city, season, body, memory, scent, love, work, escape.
- Use juxtaposition to create association between culture, product, emotion, and scene.
- Use parallel structures to create rhythm and a set of life fragments.
- Use concrete objects to wake abstract emotion: books, pens, coffee, old photos, scent, clothes, streets, moonlight.
- Turn consumption into a spiritual action: expression, escape, confession, reunion, loyalty, departure, or self-definition.
- Use light contradiction or ambiguity to add intellectual tension.
- Let event or product information land naturally from the cultural narrative.
- Keep all expressions unified by the brand's cultural personality.
- Long copy must still have rhythm, imagery, and a clear spiritual thread.

### Avoid

- Piling up cultural names to look premium.
- Using references as decoration instead of meaning.
- Making readers feel excluded or confused.
- Writing beautiful stream-of-consciousness copy that forgets the business task.
- Copying the tone of a famous brand instead of learning its translation structure.
- Using empty lifestyle labels such as tasteful, understands life, or high-quality living.

## 6. 对一个人说话：广告是说服，不是表演

### Core judgment

Advertising is not a performance for an imagined crowd. It is a clear, purposeful conversation with one real person. Copy should persuade someone to understand, trust, care, act, buy, or share.

### Use when

- Long copy
- Brand advertising
- Product advertising
- Conversion copy
- Proposal copy
- KV copy
- Video scripts
- Any work that must become effective, not only impressive

### Rules

- Imagine one specific person sitting across from you.
- Write like a real conversation, not a broadcast or award entry.
- Sell something: a belief, reason, action, trust, trial, or purchase.
- Let visuals support persuasion; do not let them become decorative intelligence.
- Copy must carry the idea, not leave all responsibility to the image.
- Judge effectiveness by asking whether the reader is more likely to understand, believe, try, buy, or share.
- Do not write for awards. If effective work wins awards, that is secondary.
- The cleverer the line, the more carefully it must be checked for clarity.
- Entertainment must serve persuasion.
- If the audience must work too hard to decode the idea, the work may be too far from the consumer.

### Avoid

- Treating cleverness as effectiveness.
- Letting awards define success.
- Turning the visual into a puzzle with no sales reason.
- Shouting at everyone and moving no one.
- Writing for industry applause instead of consumer response.

## 7. 言简义丰法：用最少文字装下最大信息张力

### Core judgment

Strong short copy is not merely brief or beautiful. It uses the fewest necessary words to carry information, emotion, image, brand task, and memorability.

### Use when

- Campaign slogans
- KV headlines
- Poster copy
- Social titles
- Brand attitude lines
- Video end lines
- Any line that must be remembered, repeated, or shared

### Rules

- One sentence should usually carry one core idea.
- Prefer simple sentence structures and direct statements.
- Cut every word that does not add meaning, rhythm, image, or force.
- Do not cut so much that the promise, subject, or emotional meaning becomes incomplete.
- Add one meaningful pause, contrast, or unexpected word only when it remains logically sound.
- Give the sentence a clear emotional direction.
- Read it aloud to test rhythm, repeatability, and memorability.
- Bind the line to a brand stance, product truth, or human value, not only to clever phrasing.
- If removing the brand or product makes the line equally usable for anyone, strengthen brand ownership.

### Avoid

- Short lines with missing meaning.
- Clever but illogical word pairings.
- Rhythm that hides emptiness.
- Abstract values without product or brand connection.
- Treating "viral" as emotional provocation instead of useful persuasion.

## 8. 缺点转资产法：把品牌短板写成可爱的立场

### Core judgment

Not every brand should pretend to be premium, cool, or fashionable. Some brands become more valuable when they admit a real weakness and translate it into a believable consumer virtue.

### Use when

- Value brands
- Traditional or regional brands
- Brand refresh work
- Practical categories
- Price-advantage products
- Brands seen as uncool, ordinary, cheap, old, local, plain, or unfashionable
- Long-term brand character building

### Rules

- Start from the brand's real situation. Do not disguise the brand as another category.
- Identify the positive value behind the weakness: cheap can become smart living, plain can become honest, old can become reliable, local can become close.
- Use self-awareness to lower resistance, but keep brand confidence.
- Translate price advantage into life wisdom, not low-end signaling.
- Build a stable voice, such as dry humor, plain honesty, restraint, warmth, or practical intelligence.
- Put promotional information inside a larger life attitude whenever possible.
- Repeat the same personality and value over time so the copy becomes a brand asset.
- Let execution vary by occasion, but keep the underlying stance consistent.

### Avoid

- Turning self-awareness into self-deprecation.
- Writing only "cheap" and damaging perceived value.
- Forced youth slang or internet tone.
- One-off jokes with no brand accumulation.
- Reframing a weakness without a real business advantage behind it.

## 9. 现代汉语广告语法法：先让句子成立，再让句子有广告性

### Core judgment

Advertising copy is not ordinary explanation. It must actively shape Chinese characters, words, syntax, tone, rhythm, context, and semantic displacement so that a sentence compresses meaning, creates pause, and becomes memorable.

### Use when

- Campaign lines
- KV copy
- Short-form social copy
- Naming and taglines
- Poster copy
- Any line that feels correct but too plain
- Any draft that has insight but lacks advertising tension

### Rules

- 字词层: Test whether the key word can be changed, added to, removed from, split, echoed, redefined, or made into a meaningful pun.
- 句法层: Use positive/negative, omission, hypothesis, choice, parallel clauses, repeated clauses, and reversal to create tension.
- 语气层: Decide whether the line should speak as reminder, confession, command, provocation, narration, dry humor, question, or intimate aside.
- 语境层: Write inside the actual context of image, medium, brand, audience, and scene.
- 创异层: Create "reasonable abnormality": the line may feel unusual, but it must become more correct after the reader thinks about it.
- Strategy conclusions cannot be pasted directly as copy. They must be re-expressed through syntax, tone, and image.
- Wordplay is useful only when it clarifies or sharpens the communication task.

### Avoid

- Word games that reduce clarity.
- Puns, split characters, or homophones as the idea itself.
- Beautiful sentences with weak product ownership.
- Lines detached from visual context, media format, or user situation.
- Copy that only restates strategy in a prettier tone.

## Quality Loop

Before finalizing copy:

1. State the task: awareness, trust, desire, action, conversion, or brand meaning.
2. Pick the relevant methodology from this file.
3. Generate multiple options.
4. Eliminate lines that are vague, unproven, over-clever, off-brand, or not addressed to a real person.
5. Rewrite the strongest options for clarity, specificity, and psychological force.
6. Recommend one option and explain why it works.
