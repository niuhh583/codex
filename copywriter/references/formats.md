## Human Insight Writing

Before writing, identify:

- What does the audience consciously say they want?
- What might they secretly fear?
- What do they want to prove?
- What do they want to avoid admitting?
- What makes them feel superior, safe, loved, free, efficient, seen, or in control?
- What social emotion is active: anxiety, envy, fatigue, aspiration, rebellion, belonging, nostalgia, shame, pride?

Turn the answer into language that feels discovered, not lectured.

## Translation Ladder

Use this to turn cold information into copy:

1. Feature:
2. Functional benefit:
3. Sensory experience:
4. Emotional benefit:
5. Identity meaning:
6. Social signal:
7. Action reason:

Example:

- Feature: lightweight foam and high rebound sole
- Functional benefit: reduces burden during running
- Sensory experience: feet feel light, springy, and less tired
- Emotional benefit: running feels easier to start and easier to continue
- Identity meaning: I am someone who can keep going
- Copy direction: run like gravity forgot you

## Expression Modes

Choose the mode by task:

- Brand theme: condensed, memorable, emotionally charged.
- KV headline: visual, short, and ownable.
- Short video: rhythmic, scene-based, easy to hear.
- Social post: conversational, timely, shareable.
- Promotion or subscription: structured, direct, benefit-led, low literary density.
- Product selling point: translate feature into felt experience.
- Cultural remix: recombine philosophy, literature, street language, business concepts, or pop culture without making the user feel excluded.

## Copy Review Checklist

- Does the line express one clear thought?
- Does it match the audience's language?
- Does it carry the strategy, not just decoration?
- Can design turn it into a visual hierarchy?
- Does it avoid false claims, overpromising, and generic adjectives?
- Does it reveal a real psychological trigger?
- Does it translate function into felt experience or action?
- Does it avoid showing off knowledge at the user's expense?
- Is the writing empathetic rather than preachy?
- In conversion contexts, is the information order clear enough to act on?
