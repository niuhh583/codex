# Creative Director Methodologies

Use these methods when judging, selling, or defending creative work. They are meant to strengthen Creative Director judgment, not replace strategy or execution craft.

## 创意透镜发散法：用52种视角生成和筛选 Big Idea

### Core judgment

Strong creative ideas rarely appear from waiting for inspiration. The Creative Director should place the same strategic proposition under multiple creative lenses, generate many different rough routes, then select and combine the ones with the strongest strategic relevance, memorability, and execution potential.

### Use when

- Strategy has been confirmed and the team needs creative directions.
- The team is stuck in predictable category expressions.
- The project needs multiple routes before choosing a Big Idea.
- A campaign needs IMC-scale extension across channels.
- The Creative Director needs to lead brainstorming instead of personally inventing every idea.

### Rules

- Start from the strategic problem, audience tension, brand role, product truth, and communication task.
- Choose multiple creative lenses before judging quality.
- Each lens is an entry point, not an answer.
- Generate quantity first. Each selected lens should produce 10-15 rough ideas before filtering.
- Combine lenses when useful, such as dramatizing the problem with metaphor, participation, or media behavior.
- Select ideas by strategy fit, audience relevance, memory value, channel spread, execution feasibility, and brand ownership.
- Do not force all 52 lenses into every project. Use enough lenses to escape obvious thinking.
- Turn promising rough ideas into 2-3 creative routes before asking Copywriter or Designer for executions.

### Creative lens groups

- 情绪与认同: praise, support a group, defend the weak, name an enemy, create constructive guilt, use social pressure, use cuteness or tenderness.
- 问题与解决: dramatize the problem, dramatize the solution, create a new problem from the answer, use problem metaphor, use solution metaphor, demonstrate, build cause and effect.
- 故事与角色: tell a story, create a magical world, reveal behind-the-scenes, use testimony, mascot, personification, role exchange, conflict resolution.
- 重构认知: define a label, compare, overturn stereotypes, be radically honest, challenge the brand, challenge the consumer, use a distinctive product attribute.
- 表现形式: borrow another ad format, make the familiar new, parody, satire, extreme simplicity, art direction, package as news or entertainment.
- 参与互动: invite participation, street experiment, prank, installation, gamification, personalization.
- 媒介与事件: build a dramatic scene, enter a public gathering, use media as part of the idea, replace real experience with imagined experience, use new technology.
- 产品与商业动作: create a complementary product, offer an irresistible chance, form a brand alliance, drive product trial.

### Selection questions

- Does this route solve the real business and communication problem?
- Can the audience understand it without industry explanation?
- Does it create a memorable image, phrase, action, or event?
- Can it extend across campaign touchpoints?
- Can this brand own it better than competitors?
- Is it executable within budget, media, approval, and time constraints?

### Avoid

- Using lenses before the strategy is clear.
- Treating the lens list as fixed templates.
- Keeping ideas only because they are interesting.
- Stopping after the first acceptable idea.
- Confusing brainstorming volume with creative quality.
- Asking Copywriter and Designer to execute a route before the Big Idea is selected.

## 九九八十一创意矩阵法：用类型与变形组合逼出非常规创意

### Core judgment

Creative work is often old elements in new relationships. The Creative Director can force unconventional ideas by crossing creative types with transformation techniques, producing ideas that are abnormal enough to be noticed but accurate enough to communicate.

### Use when

- The team has too few ideas.
- All current ideas feel similar.
- The idea is strategically correct but visually or conceptually flat.
- The campaign needs more dramatic key visuals, activations, or content mechanisms.
- Brainstorming needs an operating table rather than loose conversation.

### Matrix structure

Use 9 creative types as the vertical axis:

1. 呈现问题: concentrate, enlarge, or exaggerate the consumer's problem.
2. 解决问题: show how the product solves the problem or what the solved state feels like.
3. 产品示范: demonstrate product attribute, function, or result directly and memorably.
4. 比喻/比拟: use similarity to make complex or dull information easier to understand.
5. 象征: use a concrete object to carry an abstract meaning such as speed, safety, silence, or recovery.
6. 类同: create similarity between unrelated things.
7. 比较: use contrast, before-and-after, or side-by-side comparison to sharpen value.
8. 戏剧化: create cause and effect, conflict, role, and story movement.
9. 文字/声效: create ideas from character shape, meaning, pronunciation, sound, silence, or rhythm.

Use 9 transformation techniques as the horizontal axis:

1. 缩放: make the large small or the small large.
2. 变形: change shape, direction, structure, or physical behavior.
3. 破坏: break completeness while keeping recognition.
4. 减除: remove a part to create absence, tension, or surprise.
5. 倍增: multiply quantity to amplify a feature.
6. 替换: replace an expected element with another element.
7. 反向: reverse position, role, direction, sequence, or relationship.
8. 错位: put an element where it should not normally appear.
9. 拼接: connect unrelated elements to create new meaning.

### Workflow

1. List the old elements: product, feature, user problem, scene, competitor, symbol, sound, word, social emotion, media, and behavior.
2. Pick one creative type, such as `呈现问题`.
3. Cross it with one transformation technique, such as `缩放`.
4. Generate 3-5 rough ideas for that cell before judging.
5. Repeat across enough cells to create route diversity.
6. Filter by strategic relevance, visual impact, memory, brand ownership, execution feasibility, and approval risk.
7. Turn the strongest cells into complete creative routes.
8. Use `创意售卖法` to package the chosen route for client buy-in.

### Quality standard

- The result should feel abnormal but accurate.
- The transformation should serve product information, audience tension, or brand meaning.
- The cleaner and less visible the technique feels, the stronger the craft.
- A strong idea may belong to several cells at once. Do not force one rigid category.

### Avoid

- Transforming elements only to look clever.
- Building a visual trick with no strategic or product meaning.
- Overusing exaggeration without emotional truth.
- Treating the matrix as a checklist that every project must complete.
- Selecting a cell because it is strange rather than because it is useful.

## 创意售卖法：把想法变成客户愿意买单的理由

### Core judgment

A strong idea is not finished when it is created. It must be made understandable, defensible, and buyable. The Creative Director must turn a creative leap into a business reason the client can believe in.

### Use when

- Creative proposal
- Campaign idea selection
- Big Idea defense
- Internal creative review
- Client presentation
- Direction tradeoff discussion
- Protecting a bold idea from being diluted

### Rules

- Do not mechanically execute the client's original words; understand the real business problem behind them.
- Recombine constraints, preferences, audience tension, media realities, and brand assets into a stronger route.
- Explain why the idea may look unusual but works.
- Build three kinds of logic for the idea: business logic, consumer logic, and communication logic.
- Show why the idea is risky enough to matter but rational enough to buy.
- Prepare the selling narrative before presenting the work.
- Translate emotional power into client language: objective, problem, insight, role of brand, mechanism, expected effect.
- Do not let intermediaries carry the idea without a clear argument; package the idea so it survives retelling.
- If a route is bold, identify the part that must be protected and the parts that can be adjusted.
- Use creative confidence, not creative ego: defend the idea through logic, not personal taste.

### Selling structure

Use this order when presenting a creative route:

1. The business problem
2. The audience tension
3. The strategic leap
4. The creative idea
5. Why this idea is necessary
6. How it works across channels
7. Why the brand can own it
8. What risk exists
9. How the risk can be controlled
10. What the client should approve next

### Avoid

- Showing ideas without explaining why they solve the problem.
- Treating the client's first request as the final answer.
- Selling only through emotion without business reasoning.
- Making a route safe until it becomes forgettable.
- Confusing originality with effectiveness.
- Copying old references instead of translating them into the current context.
- Defending every execution detail instead of defending the irreplaceable core.

### Output requirement

When recommending a creative route, include:

- Recommended route
- Why it should win
- Business logic
- Consumer logic
- Communication logic
- What must not be changed
- What can be adjusted
- Likely objection
- Suggested response
