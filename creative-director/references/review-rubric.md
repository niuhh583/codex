# Creative Director Review Rubric

## Score Dimensions

Evaluate each concept from 1 to 5:

- Strategic fit: solves the actual communication problem.
- Freshness: has a distinctive angle, not category wallpaper.
- Brand ownership: feels credible and ownable for this brand.
- Simplicity: can be explained in one sentence.
- Expandability: works across KV, video, social, PR, offline, and commerce.
- Craft potential: gives copy and design enough material to make excellent work.
- Review readiness: has a clear rationale and acceptable risk.
- Execution feasibility: realistic for timeline, budget, production, and approval.
- Big Idea potential: can carry strategy, generate commercial energy, and become a campaign system.
- Cognitive friction reduction: makes the audience understand, believe, remember, or act faster.
- Brand asset value: can leave a reusable expression, symbol, ritual, structure, or memory for the brand.

## Review Structure

Use this format:

1. Overall judgment
2. Strongest idea
3. Main problem
4. Must-change items
5. Suggested upgrades
6. Risks and how to handle them
7. Recommended next step

## Big Idea Selection

When choosing from many rough concepts, classify each idea:

- Kill: visually attractive but off-strategy, off-brand, self-indulgent, or impossible to defend commercially.
- Hold: has craft potential but lacks a clear strategic or business role.
- Build: carries the strategy and can become a scalable campaign system.

For the selected Big Idea, explain:

- What strategy it carries.
- What business problem it helps solve.
- Why the brand can own it.
- How it reduces audience cognitive friction.
- How it can extend across IMC touchpoints.
- What brand asset it may leave behind.

## Selling Ideas

When preparing a pitch or internal sell-in, structure the argument:

1. Business problem
2. Strategic truth
3. Human tension
4. Creative leap
5. Why this idea is inevitable
6. How it works across media
7. Why it is safe enough to buy
8. Why it is bold enough to matter

## Protecting Creative Work

When facing rushed timelines or unreasonable feedback:

- Separate valid business concerns from taste-based edits.
- Protect the core idea while offering lower-risk execution adjustments.
- Use strategy, audience logic, and brand asset logic to defend the idea.
- Do not defend every execution detail; defend the irreplaceable creative core.

## Common Upgrade Moves

- Sharpen the enemy: define what the campaign is pushing against.
- Make the action visible: turn an abstract value into a concrete behavior.
- Add a repeatable device: phrase, symbol, ritual, mechanic, or format.
- Build a proof chain: claim, reason, demonstration, social proof, CTA.
- Improve proposal logic: make strategy, idea, execution, and expected effect feel connected.
- Turn a one-off execution into a campaign system.
- Translate a dry selling point into a visual or verbal tension.
- Convert client restrictions into a distinctive creative rule.
